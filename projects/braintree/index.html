Brand Identity design: Code as Art

As we began to work on the identity design for Braintree, we sat with a mix of employees from different departments. Braintree was pragmatic and had a developer led culture. They believed in writing great code, providing best in class customer service, and letting the product sell itself. This meant they also rejected marketing and the dark arts of advertising. Something about how powerful just this simple tiny snippet of code is.

We thought it would be interesting to embrace this ethos and elevate code and the adjacent culture into the core of the brand identity. Instead of marketing with aspirational photography, or illustration, we could talk about our products in a straight forward, honest voice. Visually, this meant we would rely heavily on typography.
